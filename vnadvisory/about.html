Founder & Principal · VN Advisory

Varadha N. is the founder of VN Advisory, with 20 years of operations experience across GCC, India, and ASEAN. Six Sigma Black Belt. MBA. He advises mid-market companies on supply chain intelligence, procurement transformation, and AI-augmented operations.

His work spans supply chain risk architecture, procurement transformation, geopolitical exposure analysis, and AI-augmented operations — with a particular focus on the GCC–India corridor and companies navigating the trade and regulatory shifts of 2024–2026. He has advised businesses across steel, FMCG distribution, precision manufacturing, commodity trading, and industrial logistics.

Before founding VN Advisory, Varadha held senior operations roles across manufacturing and distribution businesses in the GCC and India, where he led supply chain transformations, procurement restructurings, and technology deployments at operating scale. He brings that operational grounding to every advisory engagement — the work is specific, measurable, and grounded in what operations teams actually face.

How We Work

Direct. Specific. No consulting jargon.

The advisory practice is structured to deliver what mid-market operations leaders actually need: a specific answer to a specific problem, from someone who has operated in the same environment.

Limited concurrent engagements

VN Advisory works with a small number of clients at any one time. This is a deliberate structural choice — not a positioning claim. It means every client gets the principal's full attention, not a managed relationship with an account team.

No boilerplate frameworks

Every engagement starts with the specific operational situation, not a pre-built framework applied to it. The output is a recommendation specific enough to act on — not a 40-slide deck of industry benchmarks.

Candid assessment over comfortable consensus

If the answer to a client's problem is "you don't need an advisory engagement — you need to fix your data infrastructure," that is what they will hear. Engagements are scoped to what is actually useful, not what is billable.

GCC–India corridor depth

Most of the work sits at the intersection of GCC operations, India manufacturing and sourcing, and EU-bound trade flows. The regulatory shifts of 2024–2026 — CBAM, CSDDD, friend-shoring — make this corridor the most consequential for the clients we work with.
