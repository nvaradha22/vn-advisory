Public Engagements & Thinking
The conversations worth having

Beyond client engagements, we engage publicly on a small number of topics where direct operating experience and governance perspective add something to the conversation. We do not claim a speaking circuit. We share what we think, where it is useful.

Thought areas

AI ethics and governance in operations

Where AI sits inside a real supply chain, who is accountable for its decisions, and how boards should think about delegating judgment to systems they did not build. The honest version of this conversation — not the marketing version.

Supply chain process design

How operating models hold up — or fail — when the assumptions they were built on stop being true. Lean, resilience, regional networks, and the trade-offs between them. Process choices that survive change versus those that do not.

Procurement contracts and governance

Where contractual risk actually sits in a procurement relationship, what supplier governance looks like when it is taken seriously, and the gap between the contract that was signed and the operating reality that follows.

Board governance

Independent Director certification through IICA / MCA, with active focus on board-level governance — risk oversight, audit committee responsibilities, and the operating realities that make ESG more than a disclosure exercise.

We bring an operator's view to the boardroom. Most independent directors have never run a supply chain or signed a procurement contract. We have done both.

Operating Principles
How we actually work

Tell the truth, including when uncomfortable

If the answer is "you do not need to spend more on this," we say that. If the problem is organisational rather than technical, we say that too.

Do the work, not the pitch

Every engagement is delivered by the people who sold it. There is no hand-off to a delivery team after contract signature.

Measure what we promise

Every engagement defines upfront what success looks like. We report against it honestly, including when something is not working as planned.

Stay within our knowledge

We work in areas where we have direct experience. If a client's problem falls outside that boundary, we say so and refer them appropriately.
